ZIMBABWE INVESTMENT AND DEVELOPMENT AGENCY 2026 QUARTER ONE REPORT
A
s we reflect on the first quarter of 2026, I am pleased to report that the Zimbabwe Investment and 
Development Agency (ZIDA) has made significant and measurable progress in strengthening the 
investment environment, despite a challenging global economic and geopolitical landscape. This 
progress has been driven by key policy developments, enhanced investor engagement, and improvements 
in investment facilitation. The period also reflects a shift towards higher-value investments and more 
structured investment activity, while highlighting the need to strengthen conversion and execution going 
forward.
A key milestone during the period under review was the approval of the Public-Private Partnership (PPP) 
Guideline by Cabinet, which provides a clear and standardised framework for the preparation, appraisal, 
and implementation of PPP projects. This is a significant development for Zimbabwe’s investment 
landscape, as it enhances transparency, improves coordination across Government, and strengthens 
investor confidence through clearer processes and risk allocation mechanisms. The Guideline is expected 
to accelerate infrastructure delivery and create a more predictable and structured environment for private 
sector participation.
The Agency continues to advance reforms aimed at improving the ease of doing business. During the 
quarter, significant fee reductions were enacted under the ZIDA General Investments (S.I. 17 of 2026) 
and Special Economic Zones (S.I. 18 of 2026) Regulations. This downward review of licensing fees is a 
deliberate intervention to reduce the cost of entry and enhance Zimbabwe’s competitiveness, reaffirming 
the country’s commitment to being a cost-competitive investment destination and signalling that Zimbabwe 
is open for business.
CEO’s Statement
Mr. Tafadzwa Chinamo
Chief Executive Officer

ZIMBABWE INVESTMENT AND DEVELOPMENT AGENCY 2026 QUARTER ONE REPORT
A flagship achievement this quarter was the launch of the Business and Knowledge Process Outsourcing 
(BKPO) Framework in collaboration with the Ministry of Finance, Economic Development and Investment 
Promotion. By leveraging our demographic dividend, a youthful, English speaking workforce with a 93.7% 
literacy rate, Zimbabwe is being positioned as a premier outsourcing hub connecting European and Asian 
time zones. This premises based model, supported by targeted fiscal incentives, enables the country to 
tap into global outsourcing value chains and further strengthens its competitive positioning.
During the quarter under review, the Agency recorded mixed performance in investment licensing, with the 
number of new licences issued declining by 32.2% (from 214 to 146) and total projected investment value 
decreasing by 59.6% year-on-year. There was a notable shift in the structure and quality of investment, 
with a 62% increase in projected investment value compared to the previous quarter, driven by fewer but 
more capital-intensive projects.  Domestic Direct Investments  increased significantly, with contributions 
rising by 2,406% (from US$4.08 million to US$102.38 million), indicating stronger domestic involvement 
and joint venture activity. The capital structure also shifted, with capital equipment imports accounting 
for 46% of total investment, followed by foreign currency cash injections at 25% and foreign loans at 22%, 
reflecting sustained investor confidence alongside increased use of leveraged financing.
Encouraging progress was recorded in licence renewals, with total renewals increasing by 53% and timely 
renewals improving to 22%, reflecting the impact of strengthened monitoring and follow-up initiatives. 
The Agency’s international engagements continue to yield tangible outcomes. Deepened relationships 
with Kenya and Ghana through Joint Permanent Commissions, together with collaboration with UNCTAD 
on the Investment Single Window prototype, are helping to streamline the investor journey. This reflects 
a shift from procedural transparency towards the automation and integration of Government services, 
which is essential for modern investment facilitation. The development of the National Investment Single 
Window, anchored on the e-Regulations platform, is expected to further improve efficiency, transparency, 
and coordination across Government.
I would like to extend special thanks to our Principals and all our stakeholders for their unwavering support 
that have made it possible for the Agency to function smoothly and in turn achieve the milestones we 
present in this report. 
Looking ahead, ZIDA remains committed to attract, facilitate, establish investments for economic growth 
and development. We move forward with renewed momentum and a clear focus on delivering impactful 
and measurable economic outcomes , as we position Zimbabwe as a preferred investment destination on 
the continent. 
Thank you for your continued support.

The Business and Knowledge Process Outsourcing (BKPO) Special Economic Zones Framework 
The Agency developed and launched a Framework to operationalise the Business and Knowledge Process 
Outsourcing (BKPO) incentives introduced under the Finance Act No. 7 of 2025 and the 2026 National 
Budget Statement. The Framework aims to position the country as a leading regional outsourcing hub, 
leveraging its high literacy rate (93.7%), a large youthful English speaking workforce producing over 30,000 
graduates annually, and a strategic time zone alignment with Europe and parts of Asia.
The Framework is aligned with the existing ZIDA Special Economic Zone (SEZ) structure, which comprises 
four key actors: owners, developers, operators, and investors. The BKPO Framework adopts a facility based 
model, under which only qualifying businesses can be designated as BKPO SEZs. Designated entities will 
benefit from statutory incentives including duty free importation of capital goods, a flat 15% corporate 
income tax rate, a youth employment tax credit, and a 100% capital allowance deduction in the first year 
of operation.

New and Existing Legislation Impacting Investments
The following Statutory Instruments were gazetted during Q1 of 2026, and are available in the compiled 
Google Drive accessible via the following link: Statutory Instruments - Google Drive.
Exchange Control (Cotton Finance) (Amendment) Order, 2026 (No. 1), S.I. 23 of 2026
The Reserve Bank of Zimbabwe and the Minister of Finance Economic Development and Investment 
Promotion amended the Exchange Control (Cotton Finance) Order to liberalise the financing of seed cotton 
purchases. Under this amendment, seed cotton merchants are now permitted to access both offshore and 
onshore funds, use their own savings, or borrow from the local market to finance production and buybacks. 
While the purchase price for seed cotton is denominated in US dollars, the settlement ratio between foreign 
and local currency will be determined by the Reserve Bank.
This change provides greater financial flexibility for cotton merchants and investors across the agricultural 
value chain. The ability to leverage local funds and set off financing costs against the purchase price of 
cotton should improve liquidity and operational efficiency in the sector.
Exchange Control (Tobacco Finance) (Amendment) Order, 2026 (No. 2) S.I. 24 of 2026
The Reserve Bank of Zimbabwe and the Minister of Finance Economic Development and Investment 
promotion amended the Exchange Control (Tobacco Finance) Order. Under the amendments, tobacco 
buyers can now access both offshore and onshore funds for purchasing tobacco. Further, contractors 
who finance A1 and A2 tobacco growers are permitted to set off these financing amounts against the final 
purchase price of the tobacco. Crucially, tobacco buyers are now deemed “exporters,” and foreign currency 
surrender requirements will apply to their net, rather than gross, export proceeds.
For investors in the tobacco industry, the shift to a net surrender requirement represents a significant 
development for profitability and foreign currency management. The increased flexibility in sourcing 
capital is expected to boost the financing of A1 and A2 growers.
Customs and Excise (Luggage Ware Manufacturers) (Suspension) Regulations, 2026 S.I. 30 of 2026
The Minister of Finance, Economic Development and Investment Promotion introduced a duty suspension 
scheme allowing approved luggage ware manufacturers to import approved raw materials duty free. The 
list of approved materials includes trunks, screen printing inks, luggage straps, steel tubing and other dyed 
woven fabrics, containing ≥85% synthetic filaments (taffeta lining).
This approach lowers local production costs, enabling domestic manufacturers to compete more 
effectively with imported finished products while strictly adhering to the Government’s monitoring and 
transparency standards.
Customs and Excise (Printing and Packaging) (Suspension) Regulations, 2026 S.I. 31 of 2026
The Minister of Finance, Economic Development and Investment Promotion introduced a duty-
suspension framework for the printing and packaging sector, which is designed to enhance local industry 
competitiveness and support growth by reducing the cost of production. This framework allows approved 
manufacturers to import certain raw materials duty free. The list of eligible materials includes calcium 
carbonate, tetrahydrofuran, digiprime, polymers of ethylene and newsprint.
Customs and Excise (Electrical Manufacturers) (Suspension) Regulations, 2026, S.I. 32 of 2026
The Minister of Finance, Economic Development and Investment Promotion established a duty-suspension 
scheme for the electrical assembly and manufacturing sector. It allows registered manufacturers to 
import Completely Knocked Down (CKD) kits and raw materials without paying customs duty. This allows 
local players in this space to remain competitive amidst pressure from imported finished products. The 
continued application of the suspension scheme is contingent on local manufacturers utilising these 
kits for value addition. As such, manufacturers must obtain the Commissioner’s formal approval for their 
specific assembly method and, once approved, they cannot substantially alter the manufacturing process 
without seeking further authorisation from the Commissioner of the Zimbabwe Revenue Authority.

The Pipelines Amendment Act, No. 5 of 2025.
The Minister of Energy and Power Development strengthened the governance of Zimbabwe’s pipeline 
through key measures introduced in the Pipelines Amendment Act No. 5 of 2025. Construction, tree 
planting and excavation exceeding 375mm are now prohibited within pipeline reserves. Mining and 
blasting activities within 125m of the pipeline’s centre line are also prohibited. These measures improve 
operational safety and reduce risks to Zimbabwe’s critical fuel supply infrastructure, thereby providing 
greater confidence for major infrastructure investors.

Single Window (e-Registration) Service Design Workshop
In February 2026, the Agency, in collaboration with UNCTAD, convened the National Investment Single 
Window (e-Registration) Service Design Workshop in Harare. The engagement marked a strategic shift 
from procedural transparency towards the automation and integration of investment-related registrations 
and licensing processes across Government.
Building on the successful rollout of the eRegulations platform in August 2025, now covering nineteen 
Ministries, Departments and Agencies, the workshop brought together key stakeholders to strengthen 
coordination, streamline approval processes, and enhance the overall investor experience. The platform 
provides a centralised, transparent repository of procedures, timelines, costs, and requirements, improving 
clarity and predictability for investors.
The initiative reflects a broader commitment to strengthening the investment environment through 
integrated service delivery. Discussions focused on reducing turnaround times, improving inter-agency 
collaboration, and defining the scope and design of a fully integrated National Investment Single Window 
to enable seamless, end-to-end digital processing of investment applications.

Re-Registration
During the reporting period, two Investor bulletins were published. Investor Bulletin 1 of 2026 provided 
updates on statutory instruments and policy changes across key sectors, including transport, manufacturing,

entities must update their records to avoid deregistration and loss of legal status.

Research and Business Development
Research
During the first quarter of 2026, the Agency carried out the following research activities.
Manufacturing Sector Deep Dive
The Agency has completed the first phase of the Manufacturing Sector Deep Dive, delivering a comprehensive 
analysis of Zimbabwe’s manufacturing structure, performance and competitiveness. The study highlights 
key operational constraints, including unreliable energy supply, ageing industrial equipment, limited 
midstream processing capacity, and fragmented value chains, which collectively hinder the sector’s ability 
to operate at full potential. It also identifies investment opportunities across priority subsectors, such 
as steel, agro-processing, textiles, chemicals, and industrial materials. The findings are to inform ZIDA’s 
sector-specific promotion work and internal investment facilitation strategies by strengthening the Agency’s 
understanding of where targeted policy reforms would yield the greatest impact. A complementary paper 
examining the regulatory environment affecting manufacturing is currently under development and will be 
finalised in due course.
Participation in research forums and workshops 
The Agency participated in several research activities, including the WFP Zimbabwe CSP Evaluation 
Workshop, the CZI Business and Economic Outlook Symposium, the Monetary Policy Review, and the 
National Competitiveness Commission’s Global Risk Dissemination Workshop. The engagements further 
helped the Agency identify potential collaboration avenues with WFP including advancing blended finance 
mechanisms to strengthen the investment readiness of resilience programmes. This also strengthened 
the Agency’s investment climate intelligence, providing current insights on inflation trends, exchange rate 
management, reserve levels, among others; and helped the Agency better understand the country’s exposure 
to global shocks and identify emerging opportunities linked to climate change, digital transformation, 
energy volatility and shifting value chains.

national strategic objectives.
