RESERVE BANK OF ZIMBABWE — MONETARY POLICY 2026
Source: RBZ 2026 Monetary Policy Statement (27 February 2026), MPC announcements, RBZ official publications

========================================
OVERVIEW
========================================

The 2026 Monetary Policy Statement was presented by RBZ Governor Dr. John Mushayavanhu on 27 February 2026 under the "Back-to-Basics" strategy. The primary focus is consolidating currency and price stability, anchoring inflation expectations, and deepening structural reforms for sustainable economic growth.

The monetary policy framework rests on three strategic pillars:
1. Consolidating Price, Currency and Exchange Rate Stability
2. Enhancing Monetary Stability, Research, Policy and Data Integrity
3. Maintaining Safety, Soundness and Integrity of the Financial Sector

========================================
BANK POLICY RATE
========================================

Current Bank Policy Rate: 35% (maintained since September 2024)

On 27 February 2026, the MPC maintained the rate at 35%. The MPC stated that "the achievement of single-digit ZiG annual inflation provides scope for the eventual review of the Bank Policy Rate" but cautioned against "premature easing of monetary policy" that could "risk reversing the gains on the inflation front."

The MPC emphasized a "gradual and cautious, stepwise policy adjustment path" that is "data-dependent as opposed to date-dependent."

On 15 June 2026, the MPC reduced the Bank Policy Rate from 35% to 30%, marking the first rate cut since September 2024. The MPC described this as a "realignment of the policy rate to the structural shift in inflation dynamics" rather than broad easing.

The central bank also reduced the interest rate on the Targeted Finance Facility from 20% to 15%, with banks' lending to productive sectors capped at an all-inclusive rate of 25%.

========================================
INFLATION DEVELOPMENTS
========================================

Annual inflation (ZiG) has fallen significantly:
- Peak: 95.8% in July 2025
- January 2026: Entered single digits (first time in over three decades)
- February 2026: 3.8% year-on-year
- May 2026: 4.4% year-on-year
- 2026 projected average: 12.1% (per National Budget)
- Full-year 2026: Expected to remain in single digits

Monthly ZiG inflation averaged 0.4% during February to October 2025, reflecting effectiveness of tight monetary policy and proactive money supply management.

========================================
FOREIGN EXCHANGE POLICY
========================================

The RBZ continues to deepen the Willing-Buyer Willing-Seller (WBWS) foreign exchange interbank market to enhance price discovery and market efficiency.

Key measures:
- Strategic foreign exchange interventions to clear market and enable smooth flow of foreign exchange
- Enhancement of transparency and settlement discipline
- Intervention only to smooth excessive volatility
- Strengthened measures to curb parallel market activities and speculative behaviour
- Parallel market premium contained below 20% (historically often exceeded 50-100%)

Foreign currency reserves backing the ZiG: increased to over US$1.5 billion.
Foreign currency inflows: reached US$8.3 billion by May 2026 (compared with US$6 billion in same period 2025).

========================================
INTEREST RATE STRUCTURE
========================================

Lending rates following the 35% Bank Policy Rate:
- Minimum corporate lending rate: 40% (since September 2024)
- Maximum corporate lending rate: 45.6% (since September 2024)
- Overnight accommodation rate: accordingly high

Following the June 2026 cut to 30%:
- Productive sector lending capped at 25% all-inclusive rate (via Targeted Finance Facility at 15%)

Minimum deposit interest rates (maintained):
- ZiG savings: 5%
- ZiG time deposits: 7.5%
- USD savings: 2.5%
- USD time deposits: 4%

Higher ZiG deposit rates designed to encourage shift from USD to ZiG deposits/savings.

========================================
STATUTORY RESERVE REQUIREMENTS
========================================

Maintained unchanged:
- Demand deposits: 30% (both ZiG and USD)
- Savings and time deposits: 15% (both ZiG and USD)

The differentiated reserve ratios are designed to incentivise banks to promote savings and time deposits. Banks encouraged to mobilise affordable long-term investible funds for productive sectors.

========================================
CURRENCY AND ZIG ADOPTION
========================================

The Zimbabwe Gold (ZiG) currency was launched approximately April 2024. Key 2026 developments:
- Single-digit inflation achieved since January 2026
- Introduction of the "BiG 5 ZiG Banknote Series" to enhance trust, confidence, and credibility
- Gradual increase in proportion of local currency transactions
- Deliberate efforts to promote demand for local currency
- The fixed 2030 deadline for ending the multi-currency system was abandoned in February 2026; transition to mono-currency will depend on stronger economic fundamentals (higher foreign reserves, sustained low inflation, confidence in ZiG)
- ZiG-denominated term deposit facility (ZiGDtDF) introduced to manage excess ZiG liquidity and aid yield curve development

========================================
MACROECONOMIC OUTLOOK
========================================

Economic growth:
- 2025: 8.2% (revised estimate)
- 2026: 5% projected (supported by strong commodity prices, particularly gold and platinum group metals)
- 2025 growth estimate revised down from higher initial projections

The RBZ will continue to align money supply growth with real economic activity while ensuring liquidity conditions remain consistent with the inflation objective of preserving ZiG stability and ensuring sustainable economic growth.

========================================
COORDINATION WITH FISCAL AUTHORITIES
========================================

The RBZ will maintain enhanced coordination with fiscal authorities to ensure monetary and fiscal policies remain mutually reinforcing. Continued complementarity between monetary and fiscal policy is seen as vital to entrench macroeconomic stability.

Money supply management will remain disciplined, with the bank maintaining a prudent reserve money growth trajectory consistent with the objective of sustaining low and stable inflation.
