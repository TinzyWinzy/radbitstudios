ZIMBABWE BUSINESS REGISTRATION GUIDE 2026
Source: Companies and Other Business Entities Act [Chapter 24:31], CIPAZ portal, Registrar of Companies

========================================
OVERVIEW
========================================

Company registration in Zimbabwe is governed by the Companies and Other Business Entities Act (Chapter 24:31) and administered by the Registrar of Companies. The process is now fully electronic through the CIPAZ (Companies and Intellectual Property Office of Zimbabwe) E-Registry Portal.

Registration timeline: 1-2 weeks (local), a few weeks (diaspora from abroad).

Offline company registration under the old Companies Act (pre-February 2024) ended. All entities registered under the old Act must re-register on the new CIPZ (Centralised Integrated Platform for the Administration of Business Entities) portal by 20 April 2026 (SI 108 of 2025). Failure results in automatic deregistration.

========================================
BUSINESS ENTITY TYPES
========================================

1. Private Limited Company (PLC/Pvt Ltd):
   - Limited liability for shareholders
   - Minimum 1 shareholder, minimum 2 directors (1 must be resident in Zimbabwe)
   - Most common structure for serious businesses
   - Can issue shares to raise capital

2. Public Limited Company:
   - For businesses that intend to trade publicly or raise capital through shares
   - Subject to additional regulatory requirements

3. Private Business Corporation (PBC):
   - Simplified structure for sole traders and small businesses
   - Limited liability
   - Members own percentage shares (total must equal 100%)

4. Sole Proprietorship:
   - Simplest structure
   - Unlimited personal liability
   - No separate legal entity

5. Partnership:
   - Joint and several liability
   - Individual rates per partner
   - Suitable for professional practices and small joint ventures

6. Cooperative:
   - Limited liability
   - For farming groups, community projects
   - Registration fees: US$50-US$200

7. Non-Profit Organisation/NGO:
   - For charitable or public-benefit organisations
   - Must register with relevant government department

Recommendation for most SMEs: Private Limited Company (PBC) or Private Limited Company (PLC). Protects personal assets, looks professional, and facilitates bank loans and investor access.

========================================
REGISTRATION PROCESS — STEP BY STEP
========================================

Step 1: Create CIPAZ Account
Create an account on the CIPAZ E-Registry Portal. Provide full name, national ID or passport number, email address, and phone number.

Step 2: Name Search and Reservation (CR21)
Submit up to 5 name options in order of preference. The Registrar checks for identical or confusingly similar names. Approved names are reserved for 30 days.
Processing time: 2-5 working days.

Step 3: Prepare Constitutional Documents
For a Private Limited Company (PLC):
- Memorandum of Association: Defines company's name, objects (business activities), powers, authorised share capital, and subscriber details
- Articles of Association: Sets out internal governance rules including director appointments, meetings, dividends, share transfers, and winding-up procedures
- CR14 Form (old) / CR6 Form (new): Declares names, ID/passport numbers, residential addresses, and appointment dates of all directors and company secretary. Must include at least one resident director.
- CR6 Form (old) / CR5 Form (new): Declares physical and postal address of company's registered office in Zimbabwe

Common mistake: Incorrectly completed CR forms are the #1 reason for registration delays. Names must match IDs exactly, dates in DD/MM/YYYY format, and addresses must be complete.

Step 4: Submit Application Electronically
Complete registration pack submitted through CIPAZ portal:
- Approved CR21 name search result
- Memorandum of Association (signed by subscribers)
- Articles of Association
- Director details form (signed by all directors and secretary)
- Registered office address form
- Certified copies of national IDs or passports for all directors and shareholders

Step 5: Pay Registration Fees
Fees vary by company type. Government fees typically under US$100.
Payment methods: Mobile money (EcoCash, OneMoney), bank transfer/ZIPIT, online debit/credit cards.

Professional service fees for full registration assistance: typically US$100-US$300 (flat fee all-inclusive: approximately US$150 for Pvt Ltd or PBC).

Step 6: Certificate of Incorporation (CR2)
Once approved, Certificate of Incorporation, CR6, and CR14 forms are available to download. This legally confirms the company exists as a separate legal entity.
Processing time: 3-7 working days after submission.

========================================
DIASPORA COMPANY REGISTRATION
========================================

Zimbabweans abroad can register a company entirely remotely.

Key requirements:
- At least one director must be ordinarily resident in Zimbabwe (professional proxy or trusted local contact)
- 100% foreign ownership permitted (no local shareholder required)
- Documents can be scanned and submitted electronically

Diaspora-specific steps:
1. Secure a resident director (professional services firm or local contact)
2. Reserve company name online via CIPAZ portal
3. Prepare and submit incorporation documents electronically (may need notarised Power of Attorney)
4. Receive Certificate of Incorporation
5. Register with ZIMRA for TIN and BP Number (online via TaRMS or through local agent)
6. Open corporate bank account remotely (diaspora-friendly banks: Stanbic, Nedbank with video verification, NMB)

Recommended: Register with ZIDA for investment protection and funds repatriation guarantees.

========================================
POST-REGISTRATION STEPS
========================================

1. Register with ZIMRA for Tax:
   - Obtain Tax Identification Number (TIN) and Business Partner (BP) Number
   - Register for Income Tax, VAT (if turnover > US$25,000), PAYE (if hiring employees), Withholding Tax
   - Register on TaRMS Self-Service Portal at https://mytaxselfservice.zimra.co.zw
   - Obtain ITF263 Tax Clearance Certificate

2. Open a Business Bank Account:
   Required documents: Certificate of Incorporation, Memorandum and Articles of Association, director particulars (CR14/CR6), registered office (CR6/CR5), IDs/passports of directors, proof of residence for directors (utility bill max 3 months old), ZIMRA registration confirmation, board resolution authorising account opening

3. Obtain Operating Licences:
   - City/Town Council: General business licence (Shop Licence, Hawker's Licence)
   - EMA: Environmental Impact Assessment (manufacturing, mining, large projects)
   - MCAZ: Medicines Control Authority (pharmacies and drug distribution)
   - RBZ: Reserve Bank licensing (financial services, microfinance)
   - POTRAZ: Telecoms regulator (ICT businesses)
   - Ministry of Mines: Mining licences and claims

4. Register with NSSA (National Social Security Authority):
   - Mandatory if you have employees
   - Employer and employee contributions

5. Set Up Accounting Systems
6. Obtain Business Insurance (public liability, professional indemnity, stock cover)

========================================
ONGOING COMPLIANCE
========================================

- Annual Returns: File within 21 days of incorporation anniversary. Late filing incurs penalties.
- Tax Returns: Corporate tax return due within 6 months of financial year-end. Monthly PAYE (REV5) by 10th. Monthly VAT by 25th. Quarterly provisional tax on 25 Mar, 25 Jun, 25 Sep, 20 Dec.
- Notify Registrar of any changes to directors, address, or company structure within 1 month
- Maintain registered office address in Zimbabwe
- Keep accounting records for at least 6 years

========================================
MANDATORY RE-REGISTRATION DEADLINE
========================================

All companies and PBCs registered before February 2024 under the old Companies Act must re-register on the CIPZ portal by 20 April 2026. This is a statutory obligation under Section 303(9) of the COBE Act, formalised through SI 108 of 2025.

Requirements for re-registration:
- File all outstanding annual returns (with backdated fees and penalties if applicable)
- Ensure share capital correctly expressed in accordance with current monetary guidelines (ZiG/USD)
- Provide scanned copies of original Certificate of Incorporation and CR forms
- Updated Articles of Association aligned with COBE Act provisions (electronic meetings, director responsibilities)
- Verified digital details (email, phone) for all directors and shareholders
- Clear scanned copies of National IDs or Passports for all officers
- Board resolution authorising re-registration
- Shareholder special resolution agreeing to re-registration
- Affidavit of Authority from a director or authorised representative

Consequences of non-compliance by 20 April 2026: Automatic striking off and dissolution of the company. The company ceases to exist as a legal person.
