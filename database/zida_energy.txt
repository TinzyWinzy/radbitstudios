ZIDA Energy Sector — Where Renewable Energy Meets Opportunity

Zimbabwe has significant renewable energy potential. Solar: excellent potential per World Bank Solar Atlas. Wind: 39GW potential. Hydro along Zambezi river: 5GW. Internal hydro: 150MW. Geothermal: 50MW. Total installed capacity: 2,600MW (70% thermal). Actual generation: ~1,500MW. Total demand: 4,000MW per annum. Mining sector requires ~2,000MW by 2025. Government target: 2,100MW renewable capacity by 2030 (26.5% of generation). Southern Africa Power Pool (SAPP) enables access to 13 SADC countries — 340 million people, US+ economy. National Renewable Energy Policy provides tax holidays for BOT and BOOT deals, 100% repatriation of profits, duty-free importation of renewable energy technologies and equipment, guarantees for non-expropriation. IPPs can sell to ZETDC, SAPP, or Intensive Energy Users (100+ major firms, 4,000MW forecast demand by 2030). Investment opportunities: Independent Power Producers for solar, wind, hydro; investment into existing IPP projects; PPPs for energy infrastructure.
