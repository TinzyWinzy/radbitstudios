ZIMBABWE NATIONAL BUDGET 2026
Source: 2026 National Budget Speech (27 November 2025), Finance Act (No. 7) of 2025, Ministry of Finance publications

========================================
OVERVIEW
========================================

The 2026 National Budget was presented by Honorable Professor Mthuli Ncube, Minister of Finance, Economic Development and Investment Promotion, on 27 November 2025. Theme: "Enhancing Drivers of Economic Growth and Transformation Towards Vision 2030."

The budget sets the stage for the National Development Strategy 2 (NDS2) (2026-2030), which is the successor to NDS1 (2021-2025). It is an implementation tool for NDS2.

The Finance Act (No. 7) of 2025 was gazetted on 29 December 2025 and signed into law, with all approved tax measures taking effect from 1 January 2026.

========================================
FISCAL OUTLOOK
========================================

2025 Actuals:
- Revenue collections: ZiG215.7 billion (US$7.96 billion)
- Expenditure: ZiG219.46 billion (US$8.10 billion)
- Budget deficit: ZiG3.8 billion (US$140.1 million) = -0.3% of GDP

2026 Targets:
- Revenue: ZiG288 billion (16.9% of GDP, US$9.4 billion)
- Expenditure: ZiG290 billion (17% of GDP, US$9.5 billion)
- Near-balanced budget with deficit of ZiG3 billion

Fiscal consolidation efforts have paid off through prudent fiscal policies (expenditure alignment and cash budgeting) complemented by monetary policy, anchoring inflation and macroeconomic stability.

========================================
BUDGET STRATEGY: "QUID PRO QUO"
========================================

The defining characteristic of the 2026 budget is its explicit "Quid Pro Quo" revenue management approach: reducing the distortionary Intermediated Money Transfer Tax (IMTT) while compensating through increased Value Added Tax (VAT).

The private sector long criticised IMTT as a cascading tax inflating business costs at every transaction stage. The IMTT reduction for ZiG transactions alleviates pressure on production chains, while the VAT increase shifts the burden to final consumers.

========================================
KEY REVENUE MEASURES
========================================

VALUE ADDED TAX:
- VAT rate increased from 15% to 15.5% effective 1 January 2026
- VAT on imported services must be declared and paid in the currency of trade
- Introduction of Digital Services Withholding Tax at 15% for payments to offshore digital platforms (in lieu of VAT on imported services)
- Splitting of supplies for VAT purposes introduced
- Penalties and interest for failure to account for taxes in the currency of trade

INTERMEDIATED MONEY TRANSFER TAX (IMTT):
- ZiG-denominated transactions: reduced from 2% to 1.5%
- USD and foreign currency transactions: maintained at 2%
- IMTT designated as a tax-deductible expense for Corporate Income Tax purposes
- Definition of Financial Institution expanded to include Microfinance Institutions

CORPORATE INCOME TAX:
- Standard rate maintained at 24% (effective 24.72% with AIDS levy)
- Deductibility of interest payable on deposits made with financial institutions allowed (subject to safeguards including transfer pricing rules, thin capitalisation, and anti-base erosion measures)
- Domestic Minimum Top-Up Tax (DMTT) introduced aligning with OECD/G20 BEPS framework
- DMTT: Applies to MNEs with consolidated turnover exceeding €750 million, ensuring minimum 15% effective tax rate

MINING SECTOR:
- Gold royalty rate maintained at 5% (reversed earlier plan to double to 10%)
- Sliding scale gold royalty: 3% (up to US$1,200/oz), 5% (US$1,201-US$2,500/oz), 10% (US$2,501+/oz)
- Assessed loss carry forward limited to 30% for mining entities
- Capital redemption allowance aligned with life of mine
- Export tax on un-beneficiated minerals: 10% antimony, 5% ferro-chrome
- New 2% Corporate Social Responsibility Levy on coal
- Quoted price method introduced for transfer pricing of minerals (referencing London Bullion Market, London Metals Exchange, Shanghai Metals Market)
- Tiered royalties regime for mining activities

BOOKMAKERS AND GAMING:
- Bookmakers Tax increased from 3% to 20% of gross revenues (final tax)
- Tax on winnings by punters increased from 10% to 25%

OTHER TAX MEASURES:
- Special Capital Gains Tax of 20% on offshore disposal of specified assets (shares whose underlying value derives from immovable property)
- Non-Resident Tax on Interest reintroduced at 15%
- Permanent Establishment threshold reduced from 183 days to 90 days for construction/building/installation projects
- Cash withdrawal levy: progressive tiered structure (2% for US$501-US$1,000, 3% for over US$1,000)
- Rental income tax: landlords to withhold 10% of rental income from tenants liable for presumptive tax
- Presumptive tax collection strengthened through partnerships with local authorities and ZINARA
- Mandatory migration from presumptive tax to self-assessment for certain sectors (public service buses >25 passengers, haulage trucks, commercial water vessels)
- Dividends declared by building societies treated as dividends (no longer classified as interest)

========================================
SUPPORT FOR PRODUCTIVE SECTORS
========================================

BUSINESS AND KNOWLEDGE PROCESS OUTSOURCING (BKPO):
- Suspension of customs duty on specified equipment for qualifying operators
- Flat 15% Corporate Income Tax rate (subject to DMTT provisions)
- 100% capital allowance deduction in first year of use
- Exemption from Non-Residents Tax on Dividends
- Tax credit of US$1,500 per employee per annum (Youth Employment Tax Incentive)
- Flat tax rate of 15% on skilled essential expatriate staff

24-HOUR ECONOMY INCENTIVES:
- Additional tax deductions on selected expenditure during extended operational hours
- Accelerated wear-and-tear allowances on plant and equipment used in round-the-clock production cycles
- Priority access to import duty concessions for firms demonstrating expansion/productivity improvements under the 24-hour model

CUSTOMS DUTY REFORMS:
- Customs duty removed on capital equipment for beneficiation plants (with longer VAT deferment periods)
- Suspension of customs duty on selected raw materials (steel coils and plates for gas cylinder manufacturing)
- Suspended customs duty on electric public service buses (from 25% to 0%)
- Partially suspended customs duty to 10% on conventional public service buses (registered, tax-compliant operators)
- Suspended duty on semi-knocked-down kits for passenger motor vehicle assembly
- Duty on selected polyester staple fibres aligned with dyed woven cotton fabrics (40% + US$2.50/kg)

TAX RELIEF MEASURES:
- IMTT reduction for ZiG transactions (2% to 1.5%)
- IMTT designated as deductible expense
- Interest on deposits with financial institutions made deductible
- REITs tax exemptions extended to fund managers registering on behalf of pooled pension funds

========================================
TAX ADMINISTRATION AND COMPLIANCE
========================================

- Increased inter-agency collaboration for compliance checking
- Automatic reporting of tenders with mandatory inclusion of TINs in tender details
- Automatic linking of new business bank accounts and merchant wallets to ZIMRA
- Transaction details on accounts automatically transmitted to ZIMRA
- Regulations in shipping, clearing, and forwarding industry: minimum diploma level qualification, agent registration, unique identifiers; insurance guarantee bond increased from US$15,000 to US$25,000
- TaRMS (Tax and Revenue Management System) enhanced for real-time monitoring
- FDMS (Fiscalisation Data Management System) for real-time transaction verification
- Strengthened anti-money laundering monitoring of banking institutions

========================================
MACROECONOMIC FRAMEWORK
========================================

GDP Growth:
- 2025: Revised to ~8.2%
- 2026: Projected 5% (supported by strong commodity prices, gold and PGMs)

Inflation:
- ZiG annual inflation projected to decline to single digits in 2026
- Projected average of 12.1% for 2026
- Monthly inflation stable at ~0.4% during Feb-Oct 2025

Public Debt: Being managed through fiscal consolidation.

The budget seeks balance between expanding on NDS1 gains while safeguarding fiscal sustainability. Anchoring macroeconomic stability is the number one priority.

========================================
SECTOR-SPECIFIC SUPPORT
========================================

SPORTS:
- Accelerated capital allowance of 150% (spread over two years) on expenditure on sporting infrastructure
- Tax credit of US$10,000 on assessed income for corporates funding rural sports academies or youth sports development (registered under SRC)

AGRICULTURE:
- Support for cotton-to-clothing value chain
- Duty reforms on critical production inputs

ENERGY:
- Sector reforms approved (November 2025)
- Support for Rural Electrification Fund through VAT exemptions

MANUFACTURING:
- 24-hour economy incentives
- Support for local production capacity (gas cylinders, fabrics)
- Beneficiation plant incentives

WHOLESALE AND RETAIL:
- Sector reforms approved (October 2025)
- IMTT relief for ZiG transactions
