ZIDA Agriculture Sector — The Seeds of Success

The agricultural sector is a major component of total value added in Zimbabwe, the largest employer, and the country second largest source of export earnings. The sector contributes to the livelihood of over 60% of the country population, 23% of formal employment, 63% of raw materials required by the manufacturing sector, 30% of export earnings and 15% to Gross Domestic Product (GDP). Since 2019, agricultural production has risen by a compounded annual growth rate of 94.8%, from about US billion in 2019 to US.2 billion in 2023. Growth driven by cotton, eggs, soyabean, maize, sugar and groundnuts. Enabling policies: Command Agriculture, Transition and Stabilization Plan (TSP), Vision 2030, Horticulture Recovery Growth Plan. Investment opportunities: Dairy Farming and Dairy Value Chain, Horticulture, Floriculture, Coffee, Sugar, Soyabean, Leather, Cotton, Cereal Milling.
