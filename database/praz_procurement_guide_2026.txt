PRAZ PROCUREMENT GUIDELINES 2026
Source: Procurement Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe, PPDPA Act [Chapter 22:23], PRAZ official publications

========================================
OVERVIEW
========================================

The Procurement Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (PRAZ) is established under the Public Procurement and Disposal of Public Assets (PPDPA) Act [Chapter 22:23]. PRAZ sets standards and regulates public procurement and disposal of public assets in a manner that is transparent, fair, honest, cost-effective, and competitive.

Vision: "A responsive regulator, driving a modern, efficient, and effective Public Procurement system by 2030."

Mission: To set standards and regulate public procurement and disposal of public assets in a manner that is transparent, fair, honest, cost-effective, and competitive.

========================================
ELECTRONIC GOVERNMENT PROCUREMENT (eGP) SYSTEM
========================================

PRAZ implemented the electronic Government Procurement (eGP) System to enhance transparency, efficiency, accountability, and cost-effectiveness in public procurement. Launched by President Mnangagwa on 23 October 2023, with pilot phase commencing immediately.

All Procuring Entities (PEs) including Government Ministries, Departments, and Agencies were expected to be fully onboarded by January 2024.

The eGP System is a secure web-based platform enabling all public entities and bidders to conduct procurement activities electronically. It enhances competition, transparency, efficiency, and integrity in public procurement.

Supplier registration fees on eGP (per year):
- Micro Enterprise: US$50 (or ZiG equivalent)
- Small/Medium Enterprise: US$60
- Non-Micro, Small, Medium Enterprise: US$75
- Foreign bidders: US$850

========================================
PROCUREMENT PRINCIPLES (Section 4 of PPDPA Act)
========================================

All procurement officials must ensure:
1. Procurement is conducted in a manner that is transparent, fair, honest, cost-effective, and competitive
2. Promotion of competition among bidders
3. Fair and equitable treatment of all bidders, resulting in procurement contracts that represent value for money
4. Promotion of integrity, fairness, and public confidence in procurement processes
5. Facilitation of implementation of environmental, social, economic, or other policy considerations authorised or required by law

Accounting Officers are held accountable for ensuring strict adherence to prescribed procurement standards.

========================================
TENDER PROCESS
========================================

Standard tender procedure:
1. Contact
2. Identification of a requirement by the Accounting Officer
3. Budgeting for it
4. Preparing a specification
5. Advertising the requirement
6. Receiving offers or bids
7. Evaluating the bids
8. Awarding the tender
9. Signing of contract
10. Implementation

Minimum advertising periods:
- National tenders: 20 days
- International tenders: 40 days (as prescribed in Procurement Regulations, Section 19(1))

Bid validity period: At least 30 days (for RFQ method).

========================================
PROCUREMENT METHODS
========================================

Request for Quotations (RFQ) method (Section 34 of PPDPA Act):
Used for low-value procurements below prescribed thresholds:
- Goods: Below US$10,000
- Works: Below US$20,000
- Consultancy and non-consultancy services: Below US$5,000

RFQ requirements:
- Solicit at least 3 competitive quotations from reputable suppliers registered with PRAZ
- Use Standard Bidding Documents produced by PRAZ
- Clear statement of procurement requirement (quality, quantity, terms, delivery period)
- Specify bid validity period (at least 30 days)
- Provide adequate time for bidders to prepare and submit quotations (at least 3 days)
- Each bidder permitted to submit only one quotation (cannot be altered or negotiated)

========================================
SPECIAL PROCUREMENT OVERSIGHT COMMITTEE
========================================

Procurements at or above the following thresholds must be reviewed by the Special Procurement Oversight Committee (Section 54 of PPDPA Act, Section 10(5)):

Class A Procuring Entity:
- Construction works: US$500,000
- Goods: US$250,000
- Non-consultancy and consultancy services: US$100,000

Class B Procuring Entity:
- Construction works: US$250,000
- Goods: US$150,000
- Non-consultancy and consultancy services: US$75,000

Class C Procuring Entity:
- Construction works: US$200,000
- Goods: US$100,000
- Non-consultancy and consultancy services: US$50,000

The Secretariat must convene a meeting within 7 days after receipt of procurement record.

========================================
NATIONAL STANDARD PRICE LIST (NSPL)
========================================

The NSPL provides a structured and standardised pricing reference for goods and services procured by Public Entities in Zimbabwe. It enhances compliance with the PPDPA Act.

NSPL is used for:
- Budgeting and resource allocation
- Annual Procurement Planning
- Bid or tender evaluation, including price reasonableness assessment

Permissible variance: ±10% of applicable NSPL reference prices.
Base currency: United States Dollar (USD).
Updated quarterly.

If a required item is not covered in the applicable quarterly NSPL, Procuring Entities must undertake a price reasonableness analysis supported by market evidence and subject to PRAZ approval or concurrence.

========================================
TENDER COMPLAINTS AND REVIEWS
========================================

A potential or actual bidder who claims to have suffered loss or injury due to a breach of duty by a procuring entity may challenge proceedings by lodging a written notice (Section 73(1) of PPDPA Act).

Within 5 days after lodging a challenge, the bidder may apply in writing to the Authority for review by a review panel (Section 74 of PPDPA Act).

Grounds for challenge:
1. Commercial requirements of the tender
2. Technical requirements/specifications of the tender
3. Tender process and procedure

========================================
PROCURING ENTITY CLASSIFICATION
========================================

Class A: Major government ministries and large public entities
Class B: Medium-sized procuring entities
Class C: Smaller procuring entities and local authorities

========================================
PRAZ FEES SCHEDULE (SI 9 of 2026 — Fifth Schedule)
========================================

Exemption application fees:
- Class C procuring entity: US$450
- Class B procuring entity: US$1,250
- Class A procuring entity: US$1,750

Authority to conduct procurement (application and renewal):
- Class C: US$250
- Class B: US$500
- Class A: US$1,000

Bidder registration (annual):
- Micro Enterprise: US$50
- SME: US$60
- Non-Micro, Small, Medium Enterprise: US$75
- International bidders: US$850

Annual administration fees (domestic, based on contract value):
- US$10,000-US$20,000: 2% up to US$400
- US$20,000-US$50,000: 1.5% up to US$750
- US$50,000-US$500,000: 1% up to US$5,000
- US$500,000-US$1,000,000: 0.7% up to US$7,000
- US$1,000,000-US$5,000,000: 0.5% up to US$15,000
- Above US$5,000,000: US$20,000

========================================
PREFERENTIAL PROCUREMENT
========================================

Circular No. Ops 1 of 2025: Procuring entities must procure works, goods, and services from local producers and suppliers to support local industries.

Procurement of goods, works, and services from local suppliers is prioritised in line with national industrialisation policy.
